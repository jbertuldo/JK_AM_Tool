COACHING GUIDE

Before attending any coaching sessions, it is crucial to utilize the STOP Strategy, a mindfulness technique designed to help individuals effectively navigate challenging or stressful situations. The four steps of the STOP Strategy include:





  • Stop: Pause and create space between the trigger of a stressful situation and your response.
  • Take a Breath: Calm your mind and body with deep breaths to reduce anxiety and refocus.
  • Observe: Notice your thoughts, feelings, and physical sensations without judgment to gain clarity.
  • Proceed Mindfully: Choose how to respond deliberately and intentionally, considering your options, values, and goals for a thoughtful and effective action.






During coaching sessions, it is important to follow a structured workflow to ensure productive and impactful conversations:





  • Power Opening Spiel: Be mindful of your pace and intonation when engaging with advocates.
  • Take a Personal Interest: Ask how the advocate is doing to gauge their mood and readiness for coaching.
  • Topic Introduction: Start with clarifying questions, allowing the advocate to share their perspective and emotional drivers.
  • Assurance: Use positive scripting, offer feedback, encouragement, and constructive challenge to keep the advocate motivated and accountable.
  • Setting the Goal: Collaborate with the advocate to establish clear and specific goals, laying the foundation for the coaching session.
  • Reality Check: Explore the current situation and identify obstacles or challenges hindering progress.
  • Exploring Options: Encourage creative thinking, consider different perspectives, and generate new strategies for achieving goals.
  • Action Plan: Help the advocate develop a plan of action and set milestones for progress towards their goals.
  • Recap: Highlight SMART goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) and create accountability mechanisms to ensure progress is made.
